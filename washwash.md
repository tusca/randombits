# Washer Guide

Practical temperature and cycle recommendations balancing cleaning effectiveness and energy use. Heating water is ~80–90% of a wash cycle's energy — dropping from 60°C to 30°C roughly halves energy use.

## Quick rule

- **Cold (20–30°C)** by default. Modern detergents are formulated for cold water.
- **Warm (40°C)** when clothes are visibly dirty, oily, or sweaty.
- **Hot (60°C+)** only for hygiene: illness, baby items, bedding after guests, heavy stains, towels every few washes.

## By fabric

| Fabric | Max temp | Spin | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cotton (colored) | 40°C | 800–1200 | 30°C for lightly worn |
| Cotton (whites) | 60°C | 1200–1400 | 40°C usually enough with good detergent |
| Linen | 30–40°C | 600–800 | Lower spin to avoid creasing |
| Wool / cashmere | 20–30°C | 400–600 | Wool cycle only; no agitation |
| Silk | 30°C cold | 400 | Delicates/hand-wash cycle, mesh bag |
| Synthetics (polyester, nylon, elastane) | 30–40°C | 800 | High heat damages fibers and sets odor |
| Activewear / technical | 30°C | 600–800 | No fabric softener — clogs wicking |
| Denim | 30°C cold, inside out | 800 | Preserves color and fit |
| Viscose / rayon | 30°C | 400–600 | Delicates cycle |
| Down (jackets, duvets) | 30°C | 800 | Down/bulky cycle, tennis balls to fluff |
| Towels | 40–60°C | 1200–1400 | 60°C occasionally to kill mildew |
| Bedding / sheets | 40°C normal, 60°C if ill | 1000–1200 | |
| Underwear / socks | 40°C, 60°C if ill | 1000 | |
| Baby clothes / cloth diapers | 60°C | 1000 | Extra rinse |

## By dirtiness

- **Lightly worn** (1 wear, no visible dirt/smell): 20–30°C, short/eco cycle (~30–45 min).
- **Normal**: 30–40°C, standard cycle.
- **Heavy / greasy / sweaty**: 40–60°C, pre-treat stains, longer cycle.
- **Hygiene needed** (illness, mold, heavy soil): 60°C or higher — use sparingly.

## Energy-saving habits

1. **Use the Eco cycle.** It runs longer but at lower temperatures with less water — typically 30–50% less energy than a standard cycle at the same labeled temperature.
2. **Fill the drum** (~80% — a fist of space on top). Half loads waste water and energy.
3. **Cold by default.** Reserve heat for stains and hygiene.
4. **Higher spin speed** → less dryer time (dryers use far more energy than washers).
5. **Skip pre-wash** unless clothes are muddy or caked.
6. **Pre-treat stains locally** rather than raising the whole cycle temperature.
7. **Air dry** when possible — biggest energy savings in the whole laundry chain.

## Detergent notes

- Dose for load size and water hardness; overdosing leaves residue and wastes detergent.
- Liquid dissolves better in cold; powder works better on mud/clay and whites.
- Run a **90°C empty maintenance wash** with no detergent (or a washer cleaner) monthly if you mostly wash cold — prevents biofilm and smell in the drum and seals.

## Rough energy reference (per cycle, modern A-class machine)

| Temp | Energy |
|---|---|
| 20°C | ~0.2 kWh |
| 30°C | ~0.4 kWh |
| 40°C | ~0.7 kWh |
| 60°C | ~1.1 kWh |
| 90°C | ~2.0 kWh |

Values vary by machine, load, and cycle; use as a relative guide.
